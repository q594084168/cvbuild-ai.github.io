ATS Resume Keywords Guide — Beat the Robots

What Is an ATS and Why It Matters

Over 75% of Fortune 500 companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes before a human ever sees them. If your resume doesn’t contain the right keywords, it gets rejected automatically — regardless of your qualifications.

How ATS Keyword Matching Works

ATS software scans your resume for keywords from the job description. It scores on: keyword frequency (how many times terms appear), keyword placement (headings, job titles, skills section), and keyword context (used naturally in achievements, not just listed).

5-Step ATS Optimization Checklist

  1. Copy the job description into a word cloud tool — the biggest words are your target keywords
  2. Match job titles to industry standards. ‘Code Ninja’ won’t match ‘Software Engineer’ in ATS
  3. Include both acronym and full term: ‘Search Engine Optimization (SEO)’ and ‘SEO’ separately
  4. Integrate keywords naturally into achievement bullets — don’t just list, prove you used them
  5. Save as .docx not PDF for older ATS — some legacy systems can’t parse PDF text correctly
